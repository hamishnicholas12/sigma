    • The Treaty of Waitangi stands as a cornerstone in the history of New Zealand, symbolizing the agreement forged between the indigenous Māori people and the British Crown in 1840. Signed on February 6th of that year, the treaty aimed to establish a framework for governance and relations between the Māori chiefs and the British government. However, interpretations and translations of its contents have been subject to debate and contention over the years, leading to ongoing discussions regarding its significance and implementation.
